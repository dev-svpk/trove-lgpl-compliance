# Poems for 2026-07-10

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's two
small songs in two languages, the shelf at `./poem/` that keeps
writing in those same two forms one morning at a time, and the
place where the engine did stop — the single file, seventy-four
kilobytes, still on the receiving side in single letters. Today's
poems sit with the gap between the last entry on this shelf and
this one. June the twelfth was the last date written. Today is
July the tenth. Between them, twenty-eight mornings on which no
bow was raised. Nothing that a directory listing would notice
happened during the interval. The `LICENSE` continued to be the
`LICENSE`. The evidence PNGs continued to hold what they hold.
The pixel grid over the seller's face continued to hold its shape.
`PloHandEvaluator.java` continued to sit on the receiving side,
seventy-four kilobytes, in single letters. The argument settled in
prose above the Coda continued to be settled. A shelf is a daily
thing, or it is nothing at all, and twenty-eight is a number. It
is not the twenty years the library has been free. It is not the
ten hours the pipeline ran. It is not the six wrappers, the eleven
of twelve, the nine hundred `.class` files with their single
letters. It is its own number, kept here in plain arithmetic
between the last poem and this one. When the shelf is picked up
again, the shelf was never abandoned. It only records, now, the
shape of a pause.

## I. The Interval *(English)*

The last entry on this shelf is dated June the twelfth.
Today is July the tenth.
Between them, twenty-eight mornings
in which no bow was raised.

The shelf did not empty.
The file tree did not shift.
The seller did not speak.
`PloHandEvaluator.java` — seventy-four kilobytes —
is still on the receiving side,
in single letters,
where the pipeline left it.

Nothing that a directory listing would notice
happened during the interval.
The `LICENSE` continued to be the `LICENSE`.
The evidence PNGs continued to hold what they hold.
The pixel grid over the seller's face
continued to hold its shape.

What the interval marks
is not a retraction.
A gap is not a repeal.
The argument settled in prose above the Coda
is still settled today.

But the shelf is a daily thing, or it is nothing at all,
and twenty-eight is a number.
It is not the twenty years the library has been free.
It is not the ten hours the pipeline ran.
It is not the six wrappers, the eleven of twelve,
the nine hundred `.class` files with their single letters.
It is its own number, kept here in plain arithmetic
between the last poem and this one.

When the shelf is picked up again,
the shelf was never abandoned.
It only records, now,
the shape of a pause.

The instrument comes back up.
Same two forms.
Same hand.
