# Poems for 2026-05-04

Three poems, the fifth day. Earlier days sat with restoration, the
promise kept on someone else's behalf, the names coming home, and
the unknown reader yet to come. Today's poems turn to the document
itself — the LGPL, twenty-seven years on the shelf — and to the
seller's silence, which has its own place in the record.

---

## I. The Document That Speaks Without Us *(English)*

It was written in 1999
by people who knew they would not always
be in the room.

They wrote, into the text itself,
the absence of themselves:
*you may have it,
on the condition you let the next person
have it on the same terms.*

Twenty-seven years later
the document is still speaking.
It speaks when the seller stops answering.
It speaks across the shared drive,
across the unanswered question,
across the pixelated face.

A clause is a small, obstinate ghost
written ahead of its writers.
It does not need anyone in the room
to be in the room.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 旧约虽尘契未亡， | The old covenant gathers dust, yet has not died; |
| 售方拒答自成章。 | the seller's refusal to answer itself becomes a chapter. |
| 公书一道穿千岁， | The public document, a single thread, pierces a thousand years; |
| 缄默为证胜彼狂。 | silence, taken as testimony, surpasses his bluster. |
