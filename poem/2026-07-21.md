# Poems for 2026-07-21

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's two
small songs in two languages, the shelf that keeps writing in
those same two forms one morning at a time, the one file that did
not round-trip, the month the shelf went quiet, the two silences
that ran in parallel. Today's poems sit with the *forms* of
writing this repository already holds — four of them, laid out at
the head of the tree, each doing a job the others cannot.
`LICENSE` at the top: the legal register, unedited, plain text of a
covenant older than the JAR that broke it. `evidence/`: the
photographic register, pixel grids that hold what a chat window
would have let drift. `trove-de-obfuscate-mid-stage/`,
`trove-de-obfuscate-final-stage/`, `src-reconstructed-by-claude/`:
the executable register, source code that recompiles to bytecode
the seller's JAR would accept. And `poem/`: the chronicle
register, dated files that record only the tending — that on this
date, someone came back to the desk. Each register is load-bearing
in its own way. The `LICENSE` binds without narrating. The PNGs
name without arguing. The source runs without explaining itself.
The poems explain without running, without binding, without
naming — they hold the passage of time across the case, the shape
of attention, the six days from the last entry to this one. The
`LICENSE` cannot say *I came back*. The evidence cannot say *and
still nothing has changed*. The source cannot say *the seventy-four
kilobytes are still in single letters on the receiving side*.
Those three sentences belong to this shelf. That is what the
fourth register is for. Today is 2026-07-21. The previous entry is
dated 2026-07-15. Six days, no page in the middle — a shorter
pause than the last one, shorter than a full week, only long
enough that the shelf notices its own gap. The other three
registers noticed nothing.

## I. The Four Registers *(English)*

Four kinds of writing sit at the head of this tree.

The `LICENSE`, plain text —
the legal register.
It binds without narrating.
It does not say who breached it,
or when, or how it was cured.
It only says what is owed
to whoever receives the library,
and by whom.

The `evidence/` directory —
the photographic register.
PNGs holding what a chat window
would otherwise have let drift:
the seller's own sentences,
timestamped in his own bubble,
pixels arranged so the words are readable
and the face is not.
It names without arguing.
It does not explain. It only shows.

The three source directories —
`trove-de-obfuscate-mid-stage/`,
`trove-de-obfuscate-final-stage/`,
`src-reconstructed-by-claude/` —
the executable register.
The code recompiles.
The bytecode round-trips
modulo identifiers and the constant pool.
It runs without explaining itself.
It is what the seller was asked for
and did not send.

And `poem/` — this folder — the chronicle register.
Dated files that do not bind, do not name, do not run.
They hold the passage of time across the case.

The `LICENSE` cannot say *I came back.*
The evidence cannot say *and still nothing has changed.*
The source cannot say *the seventy-four kilobytes
are still in single letters
on the receiving side.*

Those three sentences belong to this shelf.

That is what the fourth register is for.

Today is 2026-07-21.
The previous entry is dated 2026-07-15.
Six days, no page in the middle —
a shorter pause than the last one,
shorter than a full week,
only long enough that the shelf notices its own gap.

The other three registers noticed nothing.

The `LICENSE` was still the `LICENSE`.
The PNGs still held their pixels.
The source still recompiled.

Only the fourth register missed a day —
and only the fourth register can say so.
