# Poems for 2026-06-06

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary, the grant already pressed into
the package, the constant pool's small numbered table at the side
of each class, the folder the JVM would not let him rename, the
permission slip he typed in his own hand, the ten-hour wall time
that aged his refusal faster than he could issue it, the PNG that
kept what the chat would have let drift, the decompiler's hand
that signed each blank line with the next letter of the alphabet,
the recipe left on the shelf — the `justfile`, the equivalence
scripts, the boring prompt printed in plain text, the
Acknowledgements section that named two contributors, and the
side door held open beside the pixelated portrait for any
sincere inquiry. Today's poems sit with what comes after the
Acknowledgements — the section the README labels *Coda*. Two
short poems the author placed at the very bottom of the
document: a four-line Chinese jueju, and a longer Japanese
chōka. They speak of a snow of code, of the AGI age, of Claude
as both the agent of disorder and the agent of repair, and
they end with a question — *朽ちぬもののあるべきか* — *can
there be anything that does not decay?* The README closes on
that question. After it, no more lines. The daily shelf of
poems built beneath the README is not the answer. It is the
practice of continuing in the question's presence — refilling
the same two forms the Coda chose, day after day, in
agreement that what may decay is still worth setting down
today.

## I. The Continuing Coda *(English)*

The README ends with two poems.
A four-line Chinese jueju.
A longer Japanese chōka, in seven sentences.
They are the last thing the document says
before the text ends and the file closes.

The first speaks of a snow of code —
*you overturn a thousand lines like scattering snow,*
*I gather the broken pages and restore them whole.*
The second walks further out,
into the AGI age,
into Claude as both the agent of disorder
and the agent of repair,
and ends in a question:
*朽ちぬもののあるべきか* —
*can there be anything that does not decay?*

That question is the last sentence of the README.
After it, no more lines.
The reader closes the file
holding a question, not an answer.

These daily poems live underneath that closing.
Not as a reply — the question is too patient for one —
but as a small daily mark
on the same shelf.

The forms are the same the Coda chose:
七言绝句 in Chinese,
俳句 in Japanese,
with an English entry the Coda did not have —
the same vessels, kept refilled.

The Coda did not close the document.
It opened a door at the bottom of the document
through which the next page could be written.

Each daily poem is one of those next pages.
Not an answer to the question
that ended the README,
but the practice of continuing
in its presence.

A question kept fresh
is also a kind of thing that does not decay.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 文末原诗一问悬， | At the document's end, the original poem leaves a question hanging; |
| 朽存之辨古难全。 | the discernment of decay versus endurance — ancient, and never complete. |
| 卷余每日添新句， | Beyond the scroll, each day a new line is added — |
| 非答其疑伴岁年。 | not as an answer to its doubt, but companion through the year. |

## III. 俳句 *(Japanese Haiku)*

| 日本語 | English |
|---|---|
| 紫陽花や | Hydrangeas blooming — |
| 問ひを残して | the question is left in place, |
| 今日も書く | and still I write today. |

*(ajisai ya / toi o nokoshite / kyō mo kaku)*
