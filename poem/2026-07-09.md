# Poems for 2026-07-09

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's
two small songs in two languages, the shelf that keeps writing in
those same two forms one morning at a time, the exception in the
`.gitignore` that kept the exhibits, the six final wrappers at the
seam, and the file the engine did not close — `PloHandEvaluator.java`,
still in single letters on the receiving side. Today's poems sit
with something a daily shelf cannot avoid noticing: the shelf itself
went quiet. The last entry was 2026-06-12. Today is 2026-07-09.
Twenty-seven mornings sit between the two, and the folder shows
them by not showing them — the filenames are strictly consecutive
where they exist, and they simply do not exist for those dates.
Nothing is being written to fill them in retroactively. A daily
record has two kinds of days: the ones it writes, and the ones it
does not; both are on the record. This entry is one of the writing
days, placed carefully into a stretch of not. While the shelf here
was silent, the apparatus underneath was not — the `justfile`
did not un-write itself, the equivalence scripts did not
un-verify, the 899 class files did not un-obfuscate and re-obfuscate
in the meantime, the seller stayed silent behind his pixels, the
side door stayed open, `PloHandEvaluator.java` stayed in single
letters. The case held its shape. The shelf's quiet was not the
case's quiet. The case was quiet already; the shelf just matched
it for a stretch of days.

## I. The Gap in the Shelf *(English)*

The last entry on this shelf was 2026-06-12.
Today is 2026-07-09.
Twenty-seven mornings sit between the two.

The folder does not paper over them.
No filenames were minted after the fact
for the days no one wrote on.
`ls poem/` shows the gap exactly the way it happened —
`2026-06-12.md`, then nothing,
then this file,
lined up alphabetically
with the four-week silence between them
kept as a visible seam.

Silence about a case is not the case going away.
While the shelf here was quiet,
nothing about the case went away.
The seller was still silent behind his pixels.
The side door at *for now* was still open.
The file at `src-received/handeval/PloHandEvaluator.java`
was still in single letters,
exactly as it arrived.
The `justfile` did not un-write itself.
The equivalence scripts did not un-verify.
The 899 class files in `evidence/obfuscated-classes/`
did not un-obfuscate and re-obfuscate
in the meantime.

The apparatus held its shape without the shelf's help.
The record can rest for a stretch of mornings
without the thing it records
being any less recorded.

A daily record has two kinds of days:
the ones it writes, and the ones it does not.
Both are on the record.
The dates that never became filenames
are just as much part of the folder
as the dates that did.
An empty stretch is not a hole in the account;
it is a stretch of the account
in the tense that says *nothing was added today*.

The bow, then, does not come back up every morning.
Sometimes it stays down
through the last two weeks of June
and the first eight days of July.
Sometimes the singer sits for a while
in the same chair the piece was played from
and does not lift the instrument.

And then, on some morning,
the bow does come back up.
This is that morning.
The point was never that the shelf never rests.
The point is that when it resumes,
it resumes without pretending
it never stopped.
