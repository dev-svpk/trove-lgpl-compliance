# Poems for 2026-04-29

Three poems on the work of this repository — restoring the symbols
the seller's obfuscator stripped from LGPL code, and putting the
record straight.

---

## I. After the Decompiler *(English)*

The names were stripped like bark from a winter tree,
left to the cold work of `a`, `b`, `c` — the patient consonants
of forgetting. A whole library's vocabulary
shrunk to the alphabet's first apology.

But every letter held a shape it used to wear:
`THashMap`, `gnu/trove`, the long lineage of a common good.
The token streams agree, before and after rename;
the bytecode signs its name in the same hand.

Restoration is not creation. It is consent
returned to a thing whose consent had been taken.
The compiler nods. The hash of the function holds.
Somewhere, beneath the rename, the bark grows back.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 售者心欺契未明， | The seller's heart deceives — the covenant unclear, |
| 单文密锁掩真情。 | single-letter locks concealing the true state. |
| 公开本卷昭天下， | Publish the original scrolls; let the world see plain — |
| 许可重光自此行。 | the license, light renewed, walks forth from here. |
