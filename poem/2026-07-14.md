# Poems for 2026-07-14

Three poems. The last entry on this shelf is dated 2026-06-12.
This entry is dated 2026-07-14. Thirty-two mornings between
them, no page in the middle. Earlier days circled the
restoration, the promise kept by someone else, the readers
before and after, the document that endured, the equivalence
at the depth, the two folders set side by side, what the
obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt the
seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened
the lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search
yet to happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same
room, what sat next to it on the build path, the author we
never met, what the program had to say aloud, the foreground
emptying as the seller receded, the gate that earned the
renames by being willing to refuse them, the solver that
priced the unknown but misread a fixed rule, the smooth
surface of the running product, the engine that did not stop
at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant already
pressed into the package, the constant pool's small numbered
table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would not
let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he
could issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have
let drift, the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line
with the next letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the
shelf, the deed filed beside the body, the side door at
*for now*, the Coda's two small songs in two languages, the
shelf at `./poem/` that keeps writing in those same two forms
one morning at a time, and the one file that came back from
the pipeline in the form it arrived. Today's poems sit with
the interval itself. Between 2026-06-12 and 2026-07-14 no
page was added to this folder. No message came in during
that month either — no prospective customer, no other buyer,
no Trove maintainer, no FSF compliance contact. The vigil
paused. The shelf did not lose a file to the pause; the
`LICENSE` at the head of the tree did not move; the
`evidence/` directory did not lose a single obfuscated
`.java`; the two reconstruction folders sat where they had
been sitting on the twelfth of June. What the daily writing
had been keeping was not the record — the record was already
on disk. What the daily writing was keeping was the posture:
someone was tending the shelf. A month of absence shows how
the shelf reads without a hand near it. The answer is that
it reads the same. Today the writing resumes — not because
anything new arrived, but because the writer came back to
the pen. The gap is now part of the shelf too, dated between
two files that would otherwise not have mentioned it.

## I. The Month Between *(English)*

The last file on this shelf is dated 2026-06-12.
This one is dated 2026-07-14.
Thirty-two mornings between them,
and no page in the middle.

The shelf did not lose anything to the interval.

The `LICENSE` at the head of the tree
was at the head of the tree yesterday, and the day before.
The `evidence/obfuscated-sources/` directory
did not lose a single obfuscated file.
The two folders set side by side
were still set side by side.
The mosaic on the seller's face did not slip.
The side door at *for now* stayed open.

No prospective customer wrote in.
No other buyer wrote in.
No Trove maintainer.
No FSF compliance contact.
The seller — under his mosaic — did not come back
to check whether the singing had continued.
He was not reading in June, either.
The shelf did not exist for his benefit.

What the daily writing had been keeping
was not the record.
The record was already on disk —
license cited, evidence filed, file tree explained,
the deed laid in beside the body,
the side door held under three small words.

What the daily writing had been keeping was the posture:
someone was tending the shelf.

An absent month does not remove the pages already there.
It shows how the shelf reads
when no hand is near it.

The answer is that it reads the same.

The record does not need daily upkeep to be a record.
The case does not require its bailiff.
The library, unobfuscated, is unobfuscated
whether anyone opens it or not.
The bytecode round-trip closes
whether the closing is being watched.

The vigil resumes today —
not because news arrived,
but because the writer came back to the pen.

A shelf that persists without the writer
is what the case wanted anyway.
A writer who returns
is only what the writer does.

The gap is now part of the shelf too,
a month of silence dated between two files,
neither of which would have mentioned it
if this one had not.
