# Poems for 2026-05-17

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise
kept by someone else, the readers before and after, the document
that endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set
side by side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over the seller's face, the polite prompt that
opened the lock, the preferred form named in the license, the
search yet to happen, and the other buyers in their unlit rooms.
Today's poems sit with the original author — the hand whose
names the seller stripped without ever having met him. The
library was someone else's gift, signed in a file header by
people the seller had never shaken hands with. To strip those
names was a small specific kind of trespass: not chiefly against
the buyer, but against the author whose credit was lifted off
on the way to the sale.

---

## I. The Author We Never Met *(English)*

The library was a gift. It had been signed.
The signatures sat in the file headers,
in a directory the seller had not made
and could not have made — `gnu/trove/`,
the name of a hand he had never shaken.

A man may dispose of what he owns
on whatever terms he chooses.
The terms of someone else's gift
are not his to revise,
and the credits of contributors
are not his to lift off
on the way to a buyer.

What we have done is small.
We have put the signatures back.
*Eric D. Friedman, and the GNU Trove contributors* —
people who never met us, never met him,
who recognized, looking at the shape of the work,
that this work had been a gift,
and had been given freely,
and had been signed.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 售非己造冒为真， | He sold what he had not made, claiming it as true source; |
| 尽削源名欺世人。 | stripping every source-name away, deceiving the people of the world. |
| 著者之名君未识， | The author's name was one he had never known; |
| 今归旧署复斯文。 | today the old credits return, and this body of writing is restored. |
