## I. The Twelve Mornings *(English)*

Between the twelfth and the twenty-fourth
the bow did not come back up.

Twelve mornings the shelf did not add to itself.
Twelve mornings the seller stayed silent.
Twelve mornings the side door stayed open
under the courtesy revocable from only one side.

Nothing forced the gap.
Nothing forbade it.

A poem from earlier on this shelf said
*every morning the bow comes back up.*

That was a promise about the mornings it does.
Not a contract about the mornings it doesn't.

The shelf has silences.
The waiting was part of the record;
the silence is part of the waiting.

A vigil can be kept in verse
and still pause for breath.

The repository did not move in those twelve days.
The pixelated portrait still held.
The LGPL text still sat at the head of the tree.
`PloHandEvaluator.java` still wore its single letters
in `src-received/handeval/`.
The screenshot of the running product still
showed the cards on the table
above the deobfuscated engine beneath.

Nothing changed.
The bow merely stayed down.

Today, the twenty-fourth,
it comes back up.

A practice does not require continuity
to be a practice.
It requires return.

The shelf is honest about its lapses
because the lapses are part of the truth
the shelf is keeping —
that someone was here,
that someone was elsewhere,
that someone came back.

Twelve mornings rest in the gap
between two filenames sorted alphabetically.
The directory listing is the record.
A reader running `ls` reads the silence
the same way the shelf does:
a date that is missing
is a morning that was not.

And the morning that is —
this one, now, the twenty-fourth —
is a morning that is.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 棚虚十二日无吟， | The shelf hollow — twelve days without verse; |
| 案在门犹守此心。 | the case still here, the door still ajar — keeping this heart. |
| 今旦重操弦上韵， | This morning, I take up again the rhyme upon the string; |
| 留空亦是一痕音。 | the left-blank, too, is a single trace of sound. |
