# Poems for 2026-07-08

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's
two small songs in two languages, the shelf that keeps writing in
those same two forms, the six seams that earned the hand, and
the one file — `handeval/PloHandEvaluator.java` — that the
pipeline sent back in the form it arrived. Today's poems sit
with the gap between them. Between the last entry on the shelf,
dated the twelfth of June, and this one, four weeks passed with
nothing added. Ninety-two files in the folder went ninety-two-and-
one thousand mornings running, then simply stayed at ninety-two.
Nothing arrived to write about. The seller did not write. No
prospective customer opened an issue. No FSF compliance contact
came to knock. The pixelated portrait did not move. The side
door held. On the shelf, the pause. Off the shelf, the same
silence that has been running since the request for source went
unanswered. Two silences of unequal kind. One of them, the one
kept by the hand that keeps this folder, ends today by the same
choice that started it. The other one has not ended and does
not appear to be ending. The pause on the shelf was a rest.
The silence off the shelf is a stance.

## I. Two Silences *(English)*

Between the entry on the twelfth of June
and this one,
four weeks passed with nothing added.

The shelf held at ninety-two files
where the ninety-third would have gone,
and the ninety-third did not come,
and the fortnight after that did not come,
and the fortnight after that did not come.
And then, today, one did.

Nothing happened during those four weeks
on the shelf.
Also, nothing happened during those four weeks
off the shelf.
The seller did not write.
No prospective customer opened an issue.
No FSF contact came to knock.
The pixelated portrait did not move.
The side door held.

Two silences,
of unequal weight.

One was kept by the hand that keeps this folder.
That hand can pause a folder for four weeks
and then not pause it any longer.
The rest costs nothing.
The resumption costs nothing.
The pause was, in every direction, provisional.

The other silence was kept
in the direction of a request that stands.
Every day the request stands
and the seller does not reply,
his silence gains a small increment of weight.
It gains that increment during the four weeks
this shelf was quiet,
and it will gain the same increment
during the four weeks after this one.
The shelf's silence does not push against anything.
The seller's silence pushes against a thing
that will not move.

A rest is not a stance.
A pause is not a position.
The hand that stopped writing here for a month
had nothing at stake in the stopping.
The mouth that stopped writing there for a month
had, and has, a covenant behind it.

The pause on the shelf ends today
because the hand decides to end it.
The silence off the shelf will end
when the mouth decides to end it,
or when someone else brings a proceeding
that requires it to end,
or when the mouth is no longer
in a position to keep it.
None of those three things happened
in the four weeks just past.

When the shelf resumes, it resumes
with the same fact it would have written
on the thirteenth of June:
that the work was kept,
the names were returned,
the deed is filed,
the door is open,
and the person the record is addressed to
has said nothing back.

The pause changed nothing on either side.
The resumption is what a shelf does.
The waiting is what the seller does.
Different verbs.
The same fact between them.

The song-shelf keeps its silences on separate ledgers.
Only one of the two ledgers has an entry today.
