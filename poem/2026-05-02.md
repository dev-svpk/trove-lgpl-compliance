# Poems for 2026-05-02

Three poems, the fourth day. Earlier days sat with what was taken,
what was given back, and the machine that did the giving. Today's
poems turn outward — to the reader who comes next, the unknown
inheritor on whose behalf the LGPL was written in the first place.

---

## I. For the One Who Comes Next *(English)*

Open the file. The names sit where they should:
`THashMap`, `gnu/trove`, the long upstream line
folded back into its proper places. Read it.
Change it. Compile it without
asking the decompiler what it thought it heard.

This is what the license was always about —
not gesture, not courtesy: a chain of competence
held open for the next pair of hands.
The seller cut the chain.
Someone else welded it shut.

You inherit a library two decades old,
written by people who wanted you
to be able to fix it.
That wanting is the inheritance.
The names are how you collect it.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 后人修码赖原真， | Those who come after, modifying code, depend on the original truth; |
| 密字千行掩素心。 | a thousand lines of hidden letters mask the plain heart. |
| 今复源流归继者， | Today the source-stream returns to those who inherit it — |
| 开源如水胜千金。 | open source, like water, worth more than a thousand gold. |
