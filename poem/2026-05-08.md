# Poems for 2026-05-08

Three poems. Earlier days circled the work — what was taken and
returned, the promise kept by another, the document that endured,
the readers before and after, the equivalence at the depth.
Today's poems sit with what this repository, in plain function, is:
the receipt the seller would not issue. Two folders side by side —
the obfuscated bytecode kept under seal, the restored names laid
out in the open — together amount to a quiet bookkeeping the LGPL
expected from him and got from someone else.

---

## I. Receipt *(English)*

He would not write one,
so the buyer wrote it for him.
Two folders, side by side:
what was sent, sealed; what should have been, restored.

A receipt does not litigate.
It does not require apology.
It records: *this was the parcel,*
*this was missing from the parcel,*
*this is what was missing put back.*

Whoever inherits the file
inherits the audit folded into it.
The next buyer who opens the same package
will find this record waiting,
patient as a clerk
at a counter no one is staffing
because the counter is the document
and the document does not need anyone
to keep standing behind it.
