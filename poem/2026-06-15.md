# Poems for 2026-06-15

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM
would not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his
own hand, the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster
than he could issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would
have let drift, the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line
with the next letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the
shelf, the deed filed beside the body, the side door at *for
now*, the Coda's two small songs in two languages, the shelf at
`./poem/` that keeps writing in those same two forms one morning
at a time, and the place where the engine did stop — the
seventy-four-kilobyte file the courtesy paused at. Today's poems
sit with what the directory listing also records — the silences.
The bow that keeps coming back up did not come up on 2026-06-13
or 2026-06-14. It did not come up on 2026-05-20, or 2026-05-25,
or 2026-05-29 earlier in the stretch. The reader who lays the
calendar over the file listing sees the gaps at once. The shelf
has not been daily. It has been mostly daily, with silences. A
cron job would have produced an entry on the mornings nobody was
at the desk; the shelf produced an entry only on the mornings
somebody was. The deed is still filed. The door is still open.
The portrait still holds. The Coda's two languages are still the
two languages. None of that needs a daily file to remain true.
The shelf is the slow add — what the writer is here for when he
is here, and quiet when he is not. The gap is part of the record.
The gap is what shows this is a person at the desk and not a
process on a timer. Today the morning came back, and so did the
bow.

## I. The Gap *(English)*

Three days the shelf did not write.
2026-06-13. 2026-06-14.
The bow that keeps coming back up
stayed down through two mornings
and most of a third.

There are other gaps further up the shelf.
2026-05-20. 2026-05-25. 2026-05-29.
A reader who lays the calendar
over the directory listing
sees them all at once.

The shelf has not been daily.
It has been *mostly* daily, with silences.

A clock would have logged a heartbeat per minute.
A cron job would have produced a file
on a date no one was at the desk.
This shelf does not.

What is not on the shelf
is the morning the writer was elsewhere —
on a long drive,
in a meeting whose minutes ran late,
asleep through an alarm,
at the desk but not in the mood for verse,
sitting with bad news from another room,
holding a phone instead of a pen.

The deed is still filed.
The side door is still open.
The pixelated portrait still holds.
The Coda's two languages
are still the two languages.

None of those need the bow each morning.
The case was settled in plain prose above.
The shelf is the slow add —
the part that says
the writer is still here
when he is here,
and quiet when he is not.

A vigil kept by a person with a life
is not the same as a clockwork promise.
The gap is part of the record.
The gap is what shows
this is a person at the desk
and not a process on a timer.

The cron would have lied on the empty mornings.
The shelf does not lie.
It writes when it writes
and leaves the date alone when it does not.

When the morning comes back,
the bow comes back up.
Today the morning came back.
Two missed dates sit beside this one
the way the empty pews sit beside the singers —
not subtracting from the song,
locating it.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 三日栏空诗未声， | For three days the shelf stood empty — the poem unvoiced; |
| 今晨复起续残鸣。 | this morning rises again, continuing the leftover call. |
| 缺笺非废皆为录， | The missing pages are not voided — they too are part of the record; |
| 人在朝朝亦是情。 | the writer is here, morning by morning — even this is feeling. |
