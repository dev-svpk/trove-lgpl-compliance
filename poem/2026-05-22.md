# Poems for 2026-05-22

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept by
someone else, the readers before and after, the document that endured,
the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by side, what the
obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the seller would not issue, the
collapsing arithmetic of repair, the mosaic kept over his face, the
polite prompt that opened the lock, the clause that had waited thirty-five
years, the key left in the same room, the spoken strings the program had
to say aloud, and the foreground emptying as the seller receded. Today's
poems sit with the product itself — a poker solver, a machine built to
reason under uncertainty, to weigh hidden cards and price the unknown.
Its maker shipped a tool whose whole craft is never misjudging odds, and
then misjudged the one term that was not odds at all: the license, printed
plainly, not a card face-down but a fixed rule on the felt. He ran the
numbers on a certainty, and played a sure thing as if it were a bet.

---

## I. The Solver and the Sure Thing *(English)*

The thing he sold was built to read the dark:
to weigh a hand it cannot see, to price
the river card still face-down in the deck,
to call the bluff and fold the losing odds.

A whole machine for living with the unknown —
ranges, equity, the long arithmetic of chance,
the discipline of never being certain
and so, by craft, of never being badly wrong.

But the license was not a card face-down.
It was the printed rule along the table's felt,
the one fixed term that does not get re-dealt:
*give back the source in the form one can read.*

He ran the odds on a thing that had no odds.
He played a certainty as a hand to win,
slipped the labels off and pushed his chips —
and lost the only pot that was never a gamble.

The solver could price every hidden thing
except the open rule it was wrapped inside.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 牌局玄机算未知， | The card-table's hidden workings — it reckons the unknown, |
| 器能料暗不能欺。 | the machine can gauge the dark; it cannot be deceived by chance. |
| 明文一纸非投注， | The plain text, a single sheet, is no wager — |
| 何苦将之作弈棋。 | why, then, did he play it as a game to be won? |
