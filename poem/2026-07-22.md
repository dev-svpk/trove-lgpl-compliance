# Poems for 2026-07-22

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the
mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the lock,
the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to happen, the
other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat next to it on
the build path, the author we never met, what the program had to say
aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller receded, the gate that
earned the renames by being willing to refuse them, the solver that
priced the unknown but misread a fixed rule, the smooth surface of
the running product, the engine that did not stop at the boundary
the seller had drawn, the grant already pressed into the package,
the constant pool's small numbered table at the side of each class,
the folder the JVM would not let him rename, the permission slip he
typed in his own hand, the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal
faster than he could issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat
would have let drift, the decompiler's hand that signed each blank
line with the next letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the
shelf, the deed filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*,
the Coda's two small songs in two languages, the shelf that keeps
writing in those same two forms one morning at a time, the two
silences of unequal kind, the interval that resumed without
pretending it never stopped, and the file the engine did not close
— `PloHandEvaluator.java`, seventy-four kilobytes, still in single
letters on the receiving side. Today's poems sit with what that file
*is*, in the seller's product. It is the hand evaluator: the
arithmetic that takes two hole cards and five board cards and
returns a number saying who wins a hand of Pot Limit Omaha. It is
the piece whose job in his program is the reveal — the routine that
ends the moment of hidden information and moves a poker hand from
concealment into the settlement that closes it. When the seller was
asked to show the source — his own hand, behind the running binary
— he declined. Twelve of his own files came in through the
pipeline; eleven went out with their upstream names restored; the
file whose function in his product is the reveal did not itself
come out revealed. In poker there is a name for this. You are asked
to show, and you do not show. The rules do not require you to. The
hand goes to the other side. The evaluator, still on the receiving
side of the pipeline, is the hand the seller was asked to show and
did not. The directory that would have held its restored version is
missing. The directory listing is the receipt of the fold. The hand
goes to the reader.

## I. The Fold *(English)*

The product the seller shipped is a poker solver.
Its purpose, at the end of a decision tree,
is a showdown —
the arithmetic that takes the hidden cards
and returns who wins.

The file that does that arithmetic
is called `PloHandEvaluator.java`.
Seventy-four kilobytes.
The core the rest of the product wraps.

When he was asked to show the source —
his own hand, behind the running binary —
he declined.

Inside the share he sent,
`PloHandEvaluator.java` was decompiler output
with single-letter identifiers,
methods `a`, `b`, `c`,
fields `d`, `e`, `f`, `g` —
the same treatment as the nine hundred Trove files
in `gnu/trove/` next to it.

The pipeline that took those nine hundred files
back to their upstream names
crossed the seam at `gnu/trove/`,
continued into `solver/`,
returned eleven files of his own work
with their names restored,
and stopped at his hand evaluator.

Twelve came in.
Eleven went out.

The file whose job is the reveal
did not itself come out revealed.

In poker, this is a fold.
You are asked to show.
You do not show.
The rules do not require you to.
The hand goes to the other side.

The customer paid one price.
The license asked for one thing.
The showdown at the end of the request for source
was the seller's to make.
He folded.

The evaluator sits at
`src-received/handeval/PloHandEvaluator.java`,
seventy-four kilobytes, single letters,
the arithmetic that ends a poker hand
kept in the arithmetic that ended a decompilation.

There is a symmetry here worth naming.
The file whose function in his game is to end concealment
is the file he kept concealed
in the conversation his game sits inside.

The evaluator would have ended a hand
by returning a number that says which side won.
The record does not return a number.
It returns a directory listing:

    src-received/handeval/PloHandEvaluator.java   74K
    src-reconstructed-by-claude/handeval/         (does not exist)

That is the receipt of the fold.
The hand goes to the reader.
