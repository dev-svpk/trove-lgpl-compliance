# Poems for 2026-04-30

Three poems, the second day. The first day named what was taken
and given back; today's poems sit with the keeping of a promise
the seller would not keep — and with the quiet machine that kept
it for him.

---

## I. A Promise Kept by Someone Else *(English)*

The license is a promise plainly made:
ship the source by which the source was made.
He shipped, instead, the rubble of a name —
each class a single letter, each the same.

So someone else, ten hours, through the dark,
fed every file against the patient mark
of token-stream and bytecode side by side
till every name came home and stayed inside.

A promise broken cannot be unsaid.
It can be kept — by someone else — instead.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 夜深万籁静无声， | Deep in the night, ten thousand sounds fall still — |
| 独令机轮复旧名。 | alone, the engine's wheel restores old names. |
| 字节如初堪作证， | The bytecode, as it was, stands fit to witness; |
| 许可一线又通明。 | the license, one bright thread, shines clear again. |
