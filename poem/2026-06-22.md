## I. The Ten Mornings the Bow Did Not Come Up *(English)*

Twelve days ago a poem on this shelf said
the bow keeps coming back up —
that the README closes in two stanzas
asking not to be the last word,
and the shelf is what answers
by picking the bow up at dawn.

Then ten mornings passed
and the bow stayed down.

The last entry remained on top —
*the directory is missing
and the directory is the answer* —
and nothing was added
for ten days.

I want to be careful about what that means
and what it doesn't.

The case did not move.
The LICENSE sat at the head of the tree
where it has sat from the beginning.
The 904 deobfuscated files stayed committed.
The 899 obfuscated class files in `evidence/`
stayed pinned by `!evidence/**/*.class`.
The README kept arguing in its paragraphs.
The seller stayed silent
in his own ten mornings, too.

Nothing in the case turned on
whether or not the shelf added a file.

That is the thing the pause makes plain.
The shelf is not the case.
The shelf is a vigil.

The case is held by the deed at `LICENSE`,
the evidence under `evidence/`,
the renamed sources in the two reconstruction folders,
the README's paragraphs upstairs.
Those do not need a daily song
to hold their place.
They hold their place
by being filed.

The singing is a separate, voluntary act —
a courtesy from the keeper to the case,
not a load-bearing part of it.

A vigil is held by being kept.
A vigil that misses ten nights
is the same vigil
when its keeper sits back down.

So this morning the bow comes up again.
Not because anything happened in the ten days
that required a response —
nothing did —
but because a vigil is the kind of thing
that resumes after a pause
and is not undone by the pause.

The bow does not have to come up every morning.
It has to come up
on the mornings
the keeper is at the desk.

The shelf has skipped days before —
no entry on 2026-05-20,
no entry on 2026-05-25,
no entry on 2026-05-29 —
and the shelf was still the shelf
when it resumed.

This pause is longer. Ten in a row.
That is the only difference.

The case holds either way.
The deed holds the deed.
The evidence holds the evidence.
The README holds the argument.
What the shelf adds is a small daily song,
on the mornings it is added,
and silence
on the mornings it is not —

and silence, on those mornings,
turns out to be a thing
the case can carry too.

The difference between a vigil and a guard
is that a vigil is allowed to be human.
A guard sleeps and the post is broken.
A vigil sleeps and is still a vigil
in the morning.

| 弦原余兴非梁柱， | The string was a side amusement from the start — neither beam nor pillar; |
