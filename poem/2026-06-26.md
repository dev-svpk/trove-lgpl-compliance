# Poems for 2026-06-26

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's two
small songs in two languages, the shelf at `./poem/` that keeps
writing in those same two forms one morning at a time, and the one
file the pipeline could not finish — `handeval/PloHandEvaluator.java`,
seventy-four kilobytes of single-letter combinatorics, returned to
its author in the form it arrived. Today's poems sit with the gap
between the last entry on this shelf and this one. The 2026-06-12
entry was followed by silence — no Qiyan Jueju, no haiku, no
recap — for fourteen mornings. Today is 2026-06-26. Half a month.
An earlier poem on this shelf — *The Bow That Keeps Coming Back Up*,
dated 2026-06-10 — said the shelf would pick the coda up at dawn
and walk it through the morning, day after day. The shelf did not.
None of the standing facts moved during the gap: the deed stayed
filed, the door stayed ajar, the seller's name stayed pixelated,
none of the three named groups came to knock. What moved was only
the daily entry, which did not appear. The shelf does not announce
its pauses. It just holds what it had, the way a shelf does when
no one is at it. A vigil is not undone by being interrupted; it is
undone by being abandoned. The keeper came back. This is what the
keeper had to say on the morning the bow came back up after a
fortnight on the floor.

## I. The Fortnight on the Shelf *(English)*

A poem on this shelf, two weeks back,
said the bow keeps coming back up
every morning,
that the vigil was the singing,
and the singing was the proof.

That part was true to the intention.
It was not true to the calendar.

The last entry on this shelf
is dated 2026-06-12.
The next entry — this one —
is dated 2026-06-26.

Fourteen mornings in between
when the bow stayed down,
the shelf held its place,
no fresh page was added,
no Qiyan Jueju, no haiku,
no small recap of the days before.

The seller's name stayed pixelated.
The deed stayed filed.
The side door stayed ajar.
None of the standing facts moved.
What moved was the daily entry,
which did not appear.

The shelf does not announce its pauses.
It just holds what it had,
the way a shelf does
when no one is at it.

And here is what is true about the pause:
the case did not weaken.
The LGPL did not relax during the gap.
The seller did not write to ask
where the daily reading had gone.
No one came to the threshold
in those fourteen days
who had not already been listed
among the three who were invited.

A vigil does not require daily verse to remain a vigil.
It requires the keeper to come back.

The keeper came back.

The promise of *morning after morning*
turns out to be the kind of promise
that survives an interruption
better than it survives forgetting.
An interruption only meant
fourteen mornings without a poem.
Forgetting would have meant
no one would have noticed
the bow was down.

It is 2026-06-26. Someone noticed.

The shelf is still where it was.
The names are still where they were returned to.
The deed is still where it was filed.
What was missing was the daily noise.
What was always there was the standing claim.

A song that pauses is still a song.
The shelf knows the difference.
