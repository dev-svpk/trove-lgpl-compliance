# Poems for 2026-05-03

Three poems, the fifth day. Yesterday looked forward to the
stranger who comes next; today turns the other way — back to the
strangers who came first, the upstream hands that wrote the
library two decades ago and let it go for free.

---

## I. For Those Who Came Before *(English)*

Twenty years ago, hands we will never meet
typed `THashMap`, `gnu.trove`, the patient hierarchies
of a library built to be useful and given away.
They could not have foreseen the seller —
the small downstream merchant who, in our late season,
would buy a portion of their gift,
strip its names, and pass it on as merchandise.

They left a different bequest in the file headers:
a license. Not a wall but an open door,
held open with a written hinge.
Whoever takes the work, takes the door along with it.

The door has lately been carried by a stranger
to be kept open for another stranger.
The names the originators chose are back
in the places the originators put them.
Twenty years on, the door still swings.
The gift is still a gift.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 廿载先贤播种人， | Twenty years ago, the worthies who sowed the seed — |
| 慷然贻库不论身。 | open-handed, gave the library without counting the self. |
| 今修旧字归原处， | Today we mend the old letters back to their original places; |
| 一脉源流入后人。 | a single current of the source-stream flows on into those who come after. |
