# Poems for 2026-06-05

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary, the grant already pressed into
the package, the constant pool's small numbered table at the side
of each class, the folder the JVM would not let him rename, the
permission slip he typed in his own hand, the ten-hour wall time
that aged his refusal faster than he could issue it, the PNG that
kept what the chat would have let drift, the decompiler's hand
that signed each blank line with the next letter of the alphabet,
and the recipe left on the shelf — the `justfile`, the equivalence
scripts, the boring prompt printed in plain text — so the next
buyer would not have to rebuild the apparatus from scratch.
Today's poems sit with the README's last short section,
*Acknowledgements*. Two entries are listed there: Eric D. Friedman
and the GNU Trove contributors, for the original library; Claude
Code, for the deobfuscation pass. The seller's name is not among
them. It is not in the file tree, not in any commit message, not
on the round little portrait inside the chat screenshots, where a
square of pixels has been kept over what would otherwise be a
face. The name is not unknown. It is held — one section higher,
under three small words at the end of *Who is the seller?*:
*for now*. Two paragraphs below those words a channel is open —
*Open an issue on this repo and I will tell you in private* —
addressed by name to a prospective customer of his, another buyer
who received the same obfuscated jar, a Trove maintainer, an FSF
compliance contact. The repository credits what it admires and
withholds what no one has yet asked for in earnest. The portrait
is pixelated, not deleted. The handle is held, not lost. A
courtesy can be revoked from either side; this one is revocable
from only one.

## I. The Side Door *(English)*

The Acknowledgements section is short.
Two entries: Eric D. Friedman and the GNU Trove contributors,
for the original library;
Claude Code, for the deobfuscation pass.

That is the whole list of names credited
in plain text at the top of the repository.

The seller's name is not among them.
It is not in the file tree,
not in any commit message,
not on the round little portrait inside the chat screenshots,
where a square of pixels has been kept
over what would otherwise be a face.

The name is not unknown.
It is not lost.
It is held — one section higher,
under three small words at the end of *Who is the seller?*:
*for now.*

Three words the size of a hinge.

Two paragraphs below them, a channel is open.
*Open an issue on this repo and I will tell you in private.*
A prospective customer of his.
Another buyer who received the same obfuscated jar.
A Trove maintainer. An FSF compliance contact.
Any of them can knock,
and on the other side
a name is waiting.

The repository credits what it admires —
Friedman, Eden, the contributors,
a library kept freely available for twenty years —
and withholds what no one has yet asked for in earnest.

Quiet is not erasure.
The portrait is pixelated, not deleted.
The chat logs are saved, not forgotten.
The handle is held, not lost.

A courtesy can be revoked by either side.
This one is revocable from only one.

If you have a reason to ask,
the side door is open.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 致谢留名前辈贤， | The Acknowledgements preserve the names of the elder worthies; |
| 售人真号尚遮颜。 | the seller's true handle still has its face covered. |
| 门虽暂掩非长闭， | The door, though for now drawn shut, is not closed for long; |
| 诚至开时可一宣。 | when sincere inquiry arrives, at the opening it can be declared. |
