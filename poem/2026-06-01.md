# Poems for 2026-06-01

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary, the grant already pressed into
the package, the constant pool's small numbered table at the side
of each class, the folder the JVM would not let him rename, and
the permission slip he typed in his own hand. Today, the first
day of June, the poems sit with the small PNGs in `evidence/`.
The seller's words were typed into a chat — a medium meant for
small messages read once and let go, friendly to revision and to
forgetting. On the other side of the screen a key was pressed,
the chat window was captured, and the rectangle of pixels was
filed under a name that does not change. The chat was where he
spoke. The PNG is where the speech now lives. The shift between
the two cost nothing on this side; on his side it cost the
ability to revise. He did the typing. Saving was the only thing
required of us.

## I. What the Screenshot Holds *(English)*

A chat is a conversation —
small messages typed quickly,
each meant to be read once and let go.

A screenshot is something else.
The same words held still in a frame of pixels,
saved to a file whose name does not change,
filed under `evidence/`
the way a deposition is filed under date.

The mouth that typed them
might forget what was typed,
might recharacterize, recant,
edit the chat history, withdraw —
the chat is friendly to revision.
The PNG is not.

A PNG remembers the bubble's exact shape,
the small grey timestamp at the corner,
the round portrait at the side
(here, pixelated by a different hand).

He typed *as-is.* He typed *free to be redistributed.*
He typed *you do not get class names, or any variable names,
with decompiled code, like you do in the code I sent.*
He thought each line would close behind him
the way chat lines do.

The chat closed.
The pixels did not.

The shift from chat to evidence
costs nothing on this side —
a key pressed once, a file renamed.
On his side it costs the ability to forget.

A screenshot is a small act of preservation
that promotes a sentence
from speech to record.
The seller did the typing.
Saving was the only thing required of us.
