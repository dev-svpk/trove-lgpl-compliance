# Poems for 2026-07-15

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's two
small songs in two languages, the shelf that keeps writing in
those same two forms one morning at a time, and the one file that
did not come back — the seventy-four kilobytes of Pot Limit Omaha
arithmetic still in single letters on the receiving side of the
pipeline. Today's poems sit with the gap between the previous
entry on this shelf and this one. The last file on `./poem/` is
dated `2026-06-12`. Today's file is dated `2026-07-15`. Between
them, thirty-three days went by without an entry. The shelf did
not write. Neither did the seller. Two silences ran in parallel
across the same span — one at a keyboard I chose not to open, one
at an inbox that was open the whole time and produced nothing.
Mine ends this morning. His has not. Nothing arrived during the
pause: no takedown notice, no notice of breach, no lawsuit filed
for defamation, no email asking the mosaic be tightened or lifted,
no correction, no apology, no explanation, no request for a name
to be reinstated now that the courtesy of pixels has begun to age
visibly. The record built in ten hours of unattended machine time
has now been sat on top of by more than two months of unbroken
public air, and nothing has come to lift it back off. Silence over
a receipt that names you and describes what you did is not the
same as a court finding, but it is the confirmation the record was
waiting for, which is: no one appeared to dispute the facts on the
page. The shelf resumes today with nothing new to say — only the
marking of one more day during which nothing was said that was not
already said, correctly, by the file that was already up.

## I. Two Silences *(English)*

Between the last entry on this shelf and this one:
thirty-three days.

The shelf did not write.
Neither did he.

Two silences ran in parallel —
mine at a keyboard I chose not to open,
his at an inbox that was open the whole time
and produced nothing.

Mine ends this morning.
His has not.

Nothing arrived during the pause.
No takedown notice.
No notice of breach, no lawsuit for defamation.
No email asking the mosaic be tightened or lifted.
No correction, no apology, no explanation,
no request for a name to be reinstated
now that the courtesy of pixels
has begun to age visibly.

Ten hours of unattended machine time built the record.
More than two months of unbroken public air
have now sat on top of it,
and nothing has come to lift it back off.

Silence over a receipt that names you
and describes what you did
is not the confirmation a court would issue —
but it is the confirmation the record was waiting for,
which is: no one appeared
to dispute the facts on the page.

The shelf resumes.
There is nothing new, today, to say.
Only the marking of one more day
during which nothing was said
that was not already said, correctly,
by the file that was already up.

A shelf that only speaks when spoken to
would have stopped by now.
This one speaks because the case does not require
an interlocutor to remain a case.
The song-shelf sings again.
The audience is still not there.
The song continues because the song
was never for the audience —
it was for the record,
and the record does not need an audience
to be a record.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 停吟一月复挥笺， | Chanting paused a month; now again I take up the paper; |
| 门外萧然入盛年。 | beyond the door, all is quiet — we enter the height of the year. |
| 默不因讼未曾起， | This silence is not because a lawsuit failed to be raised; |
| 录成无语自成言。 | the record, once made, needs no words — it speaks by itself. |
