# Poems for 2026-05-18

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the seller
would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the mosaic
kept over the seller's face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the category error at the heart of the seller's claim, the
search yet to happen, and the other buyers at their unset tables.
Today's poems sit with the part of the program that was never
silent — the literal strings the runtime had to speak aloud. The
obfuscator could strip what no one would read; it could not strip
what the program itself had to say. Error messages, JSON keys, the
constants in the pool — the spoken parts stayed visible because
the program's word had to be kept.

---

## I. What the Program Had to Say Aloud *(English)*

The variable names went. The class names went.
The method names went — silent at runtime,
read by no one but the compiler.
Stripping them broke nothing.

The string literals stayed.
`NoSuchElementException`. `IllegalStateException`.
The error the program had to confess out loud
when it could not go on.
The JSON keys, the log lines, the magic constants
the program had to speak
to any other program that was listening.

The obfuscator could erase only the silent.
What the program had to say aloud
could not be made invisible
without breaking the program's word.

A program is a strange kind of witness.
The part of it audible to the world —
the handshake words, the error words,
the small admissions when something has gone wrong —
those, the obfuscator could not take.
They were under the program's own care,
not under his.

The names were the silent part of the work.
That was what made them
look easy to remove.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 匿名千行旧迹沉， | A thousand lines of hidden names — the old traces sunken; |
| 机言之处露原真。 | wherever the machine speaks aloud, the original truth shines through. |
| 默处皆删唯响在， | The silent places — all of them erased; only what resonates remains. |
| 失名旧卷自传神。 | The old scrolls, shorn of their names, still convey their spirit unaided. |
