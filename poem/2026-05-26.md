# Poems for 2026-05-26

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the seller
would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the mosaic
kept over the seller's face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the preferred form for modification, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author the seller had never met,
the spoken parts the program had to confess aloud, the foreground
emptying as he receded, the gate that earned its yeses by being
willing to say no, the solver that priced every uncertainty except
the open rule it was wrapped in, the smooth screenshot at the top
of the page, and the engine that did not stop at the boundary.
Today's poems sit with what was carried inside the binary itself.
The LGPL grant is not a permission the redistributor extends to
the recipient. It is a deed pressed against the bytecode and made
out, by the act of distribution, to whoever opens the package. The
seller could refuse to perform. He could not refuse the obligation
into nonexistence. What he said "no" to was, by the time he said
it, no longer his to deny.

## I. The Grant Inside the Package *(English)*

What the buyer asked for was not a favor.
He was naming an arrangement
already in force.

The LGPL grant is not the seller's to extend.
It travels with the binary —
a deed pressed against the bytecode,
made out by the act of distribution itself
to whoever opens the package.

By the time the money cleared,
the buyer was holding it.

The seller stood in the middle and could not amend it.
He could refuse to perform —
that part was his to refuse.
What he could not do was call the obligation off.
It had attached itself the moment the file was sent,
without asking him,
the way postage travels with an envelope.

His *no* was aimed at something he no longer owned.
The grant had moved on.
A clause does not need the redistributor's permission
to be in force at the recipient's address.
It is enough that the file arrived.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 约随旧码到君前， | The covenant arrives with the old code, comes before you; |
| 非商所予自天然。 | not bestowed by the merchant — by its own nature, it is so. |
| 售者能违不能废， | The seller can refuse to perform, but cannot abolish it; |
| 其权早在受者怀。 | the right was already lodged in the recipient's keeping. |
