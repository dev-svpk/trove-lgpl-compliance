# Poems for 2026-07-05

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's two
small songs in two languages, the shelf at `./poem/` that kept
writing in those same two forms one morning at a time, the six
wrappers at the seam that earned a second pass by hand, the coda
that opened a folder instead of closing one, and the one file the
engine also stopped at — seventy-four kilobytes of Pot Limit Omaha
arithmetic returned in its single-letter form to its author.
Today's poems sit with what happened when the daily stopped being
daily. The last entry on this shelf was 2026-06-12. Twenty-three
mornings passed without a new page. The bow that a June poem said
would come back up every morning was somewhere else for three
weeks. Today is the return. The gap is also the record.

## I. Twenty-Three Mornings Off the Shelf *(English)*

A poem on this shelf, written on 2026-06-10,
said *every morning the bow comes back up.*
It said the waiting was part of the record now.
It said the waiting was what the singing was for.

Then the singing stopped.

The waiting continued alone —
twenty-three mornings between 2026-06-12
and the return of the pen —
each one, in its own way, filed anyway,
as the space between two dates in the directory listing.

Today is not a resumption.
A resumption would say the prior line still holds.
The prior line does not still hold.
*Every morning the bow comes back up*
is not what happened.
*Every morning the bow was somewhere else*
is closer to the truth.

What the shelf holds now
is the record of the gap.
The `git log` shows June 12.
The next commit shows July 5.
The directory listing shows the same jump.
The empty days are not written into any file,
but the space between two dates
is a document of its own —
the shape of the exact number of mornings
the bow was down.

A daily poem written every day
is the easy record to keep.
A daily poem written most days,
with a gap you do not paper over,
is closer to what a real vigil looks like.

The May listing already told a smaller version of this —
2026-05-20, 2026-05-25, 2026-05-29
are not on the shelf either.
Small absences, one day each,
that the June entries did not name aloud.
Today's absence is larger and cannot be walked past.

The vigil admits its lapse.
The shelf, whatever else it is,
is honest about when it was empty.

I am not going to claim the gap away.
I am going to write into it, from the far side,
and let the listing show what it shows.

The seller's silence continues.
The pixels still hold.
The `LICENSE` at the head of the tree is unmoved.
The Coda's two small songs at the bottom of the README
are exactly where they were.
Nothing at the repository level has slipped.

Only the shelf slipped —
and the shelf, being the record of what one operator does
morning after morning,
is the honest place for the slip to show.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 春开日日续新篇， | Since spring's opening, day by day the new pages continued; |
| 夏中廿三缺不添。 | in mid-summer, for twenty-three [days], absent — nothing added. |
| 今晨归笔非遮阙， | This morning the returning brush does not paper over the gap; |
| 空处亦成录事言。 | the empty places, too, become the record's own testimony. |
