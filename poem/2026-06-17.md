## I. The Voice Cited in the Case *(English)*

A complaint, by custom, is the complainant's voice.
The plaintiff speaks first and longest;
the defendant's words appear, if at all,
through the small lens of a paraphrase.

The README does not follow it.

Three blockquoted moments — those `>` indents
the eye returns to when it wants to know what was actually said —
and all three of them are his.

> *"The code is sold as-is, and is free to be redistributed,
> resold or used however they see fit."*

That one is wide enough to drive a lorry through.

> *"No it cannot, you do not get class names, or any variable
> names with decompiled code, like you do in the code I sent."*

That one is the concession
that what was shipped was decompiler output.

A third — a screenshot two lines lower in the page —
holds him doubling down.

Each blockquote is set off, indented,
italicized, given its own bubble of white space.
The author of the README does not crowd them.
He puts them where the eye lands
and steps back.

His own prose is connective tissue —
the section headings, the file-tree table,
a one-line gloss between the quotes.

Most of what is said *in quotation marks*
in this document
was said by the man being accused in it.

It is an unusual habit for a complaint.
Usually a complainant prefers to do his own speaking.
Here, the complainant has discovered
that the defendant said enough on his own
that the prose only had to lay it out.

The strongest case has the smallest narrator.
The narrator points at the seller's voice
in three places,
italicizes them,
and lets the document close.

A case file is supposed to be the author's argument.
This one is, in part, a transcription.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 主笔虚怀让引前， | The chief writer, in humility, gives way to the quotations placed up front; |
| 三方所记皆售篇。 | the three sections set down are all the seller's own compositions. |
| 案凭亲口同铁证， | The case, resting on his own-mouth testimony, is firm as iron evidence; |
| 不待陈词理已全。 | without need of further argument, the principle is already complete. |
