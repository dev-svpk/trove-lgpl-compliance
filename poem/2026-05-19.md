# Poems for 2026-05-19

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept by
someone else, the readers before and after, the document that endured,
the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by side, what
the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the seller would not issue,
the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the mosaic kept over his face, the
polite prompt that opened the lock, the clause that had been waiting
thirty-five years, the patient document, and the other buyers who do
not yet know to come. Today's poems sit with what happens after — the
moment when the seller is no longer in the foreground of the story,
when he recedes by his own motion and the library, the upstream
authors, and the readers yet to come continue on without him.

---

## I. The Foreground Empties *(English)*

For a season he was the figure in front —
the small obstruction the work had to be done around,
a mosaic set over a face,
a refusal kept on the record.

The foreground is a place a thing passes through.
The library remains, twenty years older than him,
patient as the first day it was given away.
The upstream authors, long gone from this work,
still speak through every restored name.

He recedes by his own motion.
He was never the protagonist —
a brief, awkward chapter
in a much longer story
about a library being held open.

The story closes the chapter and walks on.
The shelf does not need him to keep shelving.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 售者一时短章身， | The seller — for a season — only the figure of a brief chapter; |
| 原编千载尚如新。 | the original tome, a thousand years on, still as though new. |
| 此人既退无回顾， | This man having receded, no looking back is needed; |
| 源律长明照后人。 | the source-law endures bright, lighting those who come after. |

## III. 俳句 *(Japanese Haiku)*

| 日本語 | English |
|---|---|
| 五月雨や | Early-summer rains — |

| 文残る | the document remains. |
