# Poems for 2026-05-16

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise
kept by someone else, the readers before and after, the document
that endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders side
by side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the
seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the
mosaic kept over the seller's face, the polite prompt that opened
the lock, the preferred form for modification, the search yet to
happen, and the other buyers. Today's poems sit with what sat
next to the obfuscated copy on the build path: the original jar
itself, named in full — `trove4j-3.0.3.jar`, upstream symbols
intact — the file the seller had to keep so that his own
compiler would know what to call, and the file that pinned the
upstream version with confidence. He could obfuscate the runtime.
He could not obfuscate the classpath he built from.

---

## I. What Sat Next to It *(English)*

The shipped jar wore the single letters,
`a`, `b`, `c` — the seller's alphabet of forgetting.
On the build path beside it, untouched,
the original sat with its symbols intact:
`trove4j-3.0.3.jar`, the full upstream names,
the volume the closed product was compiled against.

He could not strip the classpath he built from.
The compiler needed the real signatures.
The IDE needed `THashMap` to mean `THashMap`.
Autocomplete needs a word for the thing it completes.

A lock that depends on the key being kept
in the next drawer is a lock
waiting for a curious hand.

The next drawer was the build directory.
The version was printed on the spine.
The spine confirmed the spine.
