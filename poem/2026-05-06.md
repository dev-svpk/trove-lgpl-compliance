# Poems for 2026-05-06

Three poems, the eighth day. Earlier days sat with what was taken
and given back, the document that endured, the readers before and
after, and the equivalence check that did the weighing. Today's
poems turn to the act that makes all the rest legible: publication.
The seller worked behind a closed drive; the answer is to open a
shelf where both versions of the work sit next to each other and
let anyone walk between them.

---

## I. Two Folders, Side by Side *(English)*

The remedy for a hidden thing
is a place where it cannot be hidden.

Not a courtroom — those are slow,
and the seller has cleared the room before.

A repository, instead.
Two folders side by side:
what he said was source,
and what source actually is.

No argument; only the listing.
The work after that is done by anyone
willing to open both folders
and read.

A public shelf is a small, plain instrument.
You set the two things on it.
You step away.
The shelf does the rest.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 售者藏踪掩素真， | The seller hid his tracks and concealed the plain truth; |
| 公开双卷示来人。 | we open the two volumes, displayed for those who come. |
| 表深一致无可遁， | Surface and depth agree — there is nowhere left to flee; |
| 许可如灯照后尘。 | the license, like a lamp, lights the road of those who follow. |

| 日本語 | English |
