# Poems for 2026-05-10

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the machine that
did it, the readers before and after, the document that endured, the
equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by side, what the
obfuscator could not reach, and the receipt the seller would not
issue. Today's poems sit with the small mosaic over a small face —
the pixelation that keeps the seller's name held back, available but
unspoken, a courtesy with a condition attached.

---

## I. The Pixelated Face *(English)*

The face in the screenshot is blurred,
but the words below it remain clear.
A small mosaic, set over a small mouth,
holds back what the document could otherwise say plainly.

This is restraint, not absence.
The name sits in the file
the way a key sits in a drawer
that opens when the right person needs to open it.

A buyer of the same JAR comes asking — the drawer opens.
A maintainer comes asking — the drawer opens.
For everyone else
the drawer can stay closed
and the record can stay quiet.

The mercy is conditional.
The condition is not difficult.
Stop selling other people's gifts
behind a single letter,
and the drawer never opens at all.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 像中遮面字昭然， | In the picture the face is veiled, yet the words shine out plainly; |
| 姓字封函待要传。 | the surname is sealed in a coffer, awaiting those it must reach. |
| 若有求人锁堪启， | Should one who needs it come asking, the lock may yet be opened; |
| 售贾改弦永无言。 | should the seller change his tune, no word need ever be spoken. |
