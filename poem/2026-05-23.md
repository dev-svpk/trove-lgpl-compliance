# Poems for 2026-05-23

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the seller
would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the mosaic
kept over the seller's face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the preferred form for modification, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author the seller had never met,
what the program had to say aloud, and the foreground emptying as
he receded. Today's poems sit with the screenshot at the top of
the README — the seller's poker solver, mid-calculation, running
smoothly with nine hundred anonymous classes inside it. The runtime
did not need names to compute. The customer did not need names to
play. The license was the only part of the work that did, and the
license was the easiest part to defer.

---

## I. The Smooth Surface *(English)*

The screenshot at the top of the page
is the seller's product, working —
a poker solver mid-calculation,
bar charts even, tooltips polished.

Nothing in the running program betrays
that beneath the smoothness,
nine hundred classes wear the alphabet
where their names used to be.

The runtime does not care what its variables are called.
A `THashMap` answers to `a`
the same way it answers to `THashMap`.
The customer plays. The equity prints.

This was the seller's quiet luck:
the part the LGPL asked him to share
is the part no one needs in order to play.
The customer was content.

That contentment was the cover
the obligation hid inside.
