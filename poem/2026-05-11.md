# Poems for 2026-05-11

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise
kept by someone else, the readers before and after, the document
that endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set
side by side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
and the small mosaic kept over the seller's face. Today's poems
sit with the smallness of the instrument itself — the prompt
that undid the lock was not clever. It was the voice you would
use asking a colleague to tidy a pull request before lunch,
repeated nine hundred times, gated by a dumb script that asked
only whether the bytecode round-tripped. The wrong was ordinary.
The remedy was ordinary. The remedy outlasted the wrong.

---

## I. The Polite Instruction *(English)*

The prompt that did the work was nothing clever:
*please refactor variable, function, class names.*
The voice you would use asking a colleague
to clean up a pull request before lunch.

No breakthrough. No bespoke key.
A plain request, repeated nine hundred times,
each repetition gated by a script that asked
only the smallest possible question:
*did the bytecode round-trip, modulo names?*

The seller's lock turned out to be
this kind of lock —
the kind that opens for a courteous voice
and a patient diff, unattended,
through the short hours of a summer night.

There is no glamour in it.
There was no glamour in the wrong, either.
On both sides, small ordinary acts:
his stripping, our restoring, repeated.
The small ordinary acts on this side
have outlasted the small ordinary acts on his.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 何须妙策破重关， | What need of clever schemes to break the heavy gate — |
| 一句寻常自启缄。 | a single ordinary sentence opens the seal of itself. |
| 机走九百无声夜， | The engine walks nine hundred files through the soundless night; |
| 旧名归处旦光还。 | the old names back in their places, and the morning light returns. |

## III. 俳句 *(Japanese Haiku)*

| 日本語 | English |
