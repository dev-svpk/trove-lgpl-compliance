# Poems for 2026-05-14

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise
kept by someone else, the readers before and after, the document
that endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set
side by side, what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over the seller's face, and the polite prompt
that opened the lock. Today's poems sit with the strangers I
have never met — the other buyers of the same JAR, sitting on
the same envelope of single letters, not knowing yet that what
they received was not source. This repository is, among other
things, a chair drawn out for them at a table they did not know
was set.

---

## I. The Other Buyers *(English)*

Somewhere — I don't know how many, or where —
others opened the same shared drive,
downloaded the same JAR,
accepted the same envelope of single letters
filed under *full source code.*

They have not looked inside.
The program runs; why would they look?
The seller is on to his next sale,
and their folder sits closed
on a small desktop, under a small evening lamp.

This repository is for them
though most of them will never see it.
It is the message left
in the chair they were meant to sit at,
at a table they did not know was set.

If one of them ever notices,
the issue tracker is open.
The chair will be drawn out.
The name I keep behind a mosaic
will be set down beside their plate —
exactly for this.

|---|---|
| 异国同购数家人， | In other lands, several households who made the same purchase — |
