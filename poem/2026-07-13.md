# Poems for 2026-07-13

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's two
small songs in two languages, the shelf at `./poem/` that keeps
writing in those same two forms one morning at a time, the
diagnosis under the Coda — that the obfuscator used a model too
and the whole exchange was between two operators of a common
engine — and the place where the engine did stop: one file of
seventy-four kilobytes, `PloHandEvaluator.java` under
`handeval/`, still in single letters on the receiving side, one
directory the second reconstruction folder does not contain.
Today's poems sit with the interval — the thirty-one mornings
between the previous entry on this shelf and this one. Nothing on
the case has moved. The seller has not answered. The missing
`handeval/` folder is still missing. The pixelated portrait still
holds. The side door is still open under *for now*. What changed
is the light. Plum-rain gave way to cicadas; June turned to July;
the peak of summer clouds that stood over the shelf a month ago
has thickened into full-summer heat. The shelf that writes daily
did not write for thirty-one days. That absence is on the record
too. The vigil is the standing at the desk. The writing is what
falls out when the standing goes long enough. For a month the
standing was silent. Today the writing comes back — to the same
case, the same courtesy, the same directory still missing, and a
month of the world that went past without touching any of it.

## I. The Month the Shelf Went Quiet *(English)*

Thirty-one mornings without a line.
Not because the case had moved.
Not because the seller had answered.

The last entry sat where it was left —
the missing `handeval/`, seventy-four kilobytes
in single letters, one folder unreturned,
the near edge of the pipeline still holding its shape.

June ended. July began.
The plum-rain gave way to cicadas.
High summer entered the record
through nothing being written on it.

A shelf that writes daily
also writes when it does not.
The gap is a shape. The gap says: *still.*
Still the pixelated portrait.
Still the file that would not round-trip.
Still the seller who did not send.
Still the covenant sitting at the head of the tree,
plain text, unedited, waiting to be read
by someone with reason to open it.

The vigil is not the writing.
The vigil is the standing at the desk.
The writing is what falls out
when the standing goes long enough.

For a month the standing was silent.
The desk did not move.
The folder did not fill.
The threshold kept its person.
The person did not sing.

Today the writing comes back.

Nothing has changed but the light.
That is exactly what needs to be written down —
that in the interval between June 12 and July 13
the argument stayed where the argument left it,
license cited, deed filed, side door open,
and a whole month of the world
went past without touching any of it.

The bow that keeps coming back up
took a month to come back up.
It is up now.
It is not sure how long it will hold
before it goes down again.
It is only sure of what it will find
when it lifts:

the same case, waiting;
the same courtesy, still revocable
from only one side;
the same directory, missing;
the same shelf, ready
for one more small song
in the two forms the Coda chose.
