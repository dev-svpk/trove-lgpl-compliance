# Poems for 2026-06-23

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's
two small songs in two languages, the shelf at `./poem/` that
keeps writing in those same two forms, the bow that keeps coming
back up morning after morning, and the place where the engine did
stop — one file, seventy-four kilobytes, at the door of the hand
evaluator. Today's poems sit with the gap on the shelf itself.
The most recent entry before this one is dated 2026-06-12. Today
is 2026-06-23. Eleven mornings in between have no entry. There is
no reason printed for the gap; the repository does not log silence.
Git records what is added, not what isn't, and the eleven mornings
exist only in the difference between two filenames in the directory
listing. The 2026-06-10 entry on this shelf wrote *every morning
the bow comes back up* — a sentence that assumed a steady rhythm
the shelf has not, in fact, maintained. A vigil is not a metronome.
What returns today is not *the discipline.* Discipline is a word
for what hasn't broken. What returns is whoever was tending the
shelf, finding it where it was left, opening the file for today,
and writing the next entry. The waiting itself goes unrecorded —
no commits, no timestamps, no notes — until the work resumes and
remarks, briefly, that it had been waiting.

## I. The Eleven Mornings *(English)*

The most recent entry on this shelf
before this one is dated 2026-06-12.
Today is 2026-06-23.
Eleven mornings in between
have no entry.

There is no reason printed for the gap.
The repository does not log silence.
Git records what is added, not what isn't.
The eleven mornings exist
only in the difference between two filenames
in the directory listing.

On 2026-06-10 an entry on this shelf said
*every morning the bow comes back up.*
It said *the waiting is part of the record now,
the waiting is what the singing is for.*

That sentence assumed a steady rhythm
the shelf has not, in fact, maintained.

A vigil is not a metronome.
Sometimes it goes quiet for a stretch.
Sometimes it returns.

What happened in those eleven mornings —
whether anyone knocked at the side door,
whether the seller said anything,
whether another buyer found the repo,
whether a Trove maintainer or an FSF contact
sent a note —
the shelf does not say.

It only says:
the twelfth, and then the twenty-third,
with eleven blanks between.

A daily folder that admits its gaps
is more honest than one that pretends to none.
The silence has a shape.
It is the shape of eleven empty mornings
between two filled ones,
and it is part of the record now
in the same way the entries are part of the record —
by being visible in the listing,
by being countable,
by being something a reader
who scrolls the folder
will notice without being told.

What returns today is not *the discipline.*
Discipline is a word for what hasn't broken.
What returns is whoever was tending the shelf,
finding it where it was left,
opening the file for today,
and writing the next entry.

The work waits.
The waiting itself goes unrecorded —
no commits, no timestamps, no notes —
until the work resumes
and remarks, briefly,
that it had been waiting.
