# Poems for 2026-05-13

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders side by side,
what the obfuscator could not reach, the receipt the seller would
not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair, the small mosaic
kept over his face, and the ordinariness of the instrument that
opened the lock. Today's poems sit with the reader who has not yet
arrived — the next buyer of the same JAR, whose search has not yet
been typed, whose small late suspicion is not yet known to them.
The document waits without effort. It needs nothing from us to keep
waiting.

---

## I. The Search Yet to Happen *(English)*

Somewhere a buyer has not yet typed
the phrase that will bring them here.
They have unzipped the share.
They have opened a folder called `gnu/trove/`
and read the alphabet's first letters
running across the names of every file:
the small, late suspicion of someone
who has paid for the thing
that is not the thing.

They will try the phrases in turn.
*Trove single-letter source.*
*Obfuscated LGPL.*
*Seller refused to provide source.*

This document is patient.
It is in no hurry.
It will be here when they search.
It will be here if they do not search.
You build a thing like this once,
leave it standing on a public shelf,
and it asks nothing of you afterward —
not the watching, not the maintenance,
not the small dignity of being read.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 售贾如初卷未停， | The merchant goes on as before — the scroll-sales do not stop; |
| 新人启匣自疑惊。 | a new buyer opens the box and startles in his own doubt. |
| 但能一字搜寻处， | Wherever a single letter can yet be searched, |
