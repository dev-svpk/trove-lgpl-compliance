# Poems for 2026-06-21

Three poems. Earlier days circled the restoration, the promise kept
by someone else, the readers before and after, the document that
endured, the equivalence at the depth, the two folders set side by
side, what the obfuscator could not reach in structure, the receipt
the seller would not issue, the collapsing arithmetic of repair,
the mosaic kept over his face, the polite prompt that opened the
lock, the *preferred form for modification*, the search yet to
happen, the other buyers, the key kept in the same room, what sat
next to it on the build path, the author we never met, what the
program had to say aloud, the foreground emptying as the seller
receded, the gate that earned the renames by being willing to
refuse them, the solver that priced the unknown but misread a
fixed rule, the smooth surface of the running product, the engine
that did not stop at the boundary the seller had drawn, the grant
already pressed into the package, the constant pool's small
numbered table at the side of each class, the folder the JVM would
not let him rename, the permission slip he typed in his own hand,
the ten-hour wall time that aged his refusal faster than he could
issue it, the PNG that kept what the chat would have let drift,
the decompiler's hand that signed each blank line with the next
letter of the alphabet, the recipe left on the shelf, the deed
filed beside the body, the side door at *for now*, the Coda's
two small songs in two languages, the shelf at `./poem/` that
keeps writing in those same two forms one morning at a time, and
the one large file the engine could not return — the seventy-four
kilobyte hand evaluator at the door of `handeval/`, left in
single letters. Then nine mornings went by without an entry on
the shelf. The repository did not move. The 899 class files in
`evidence/` stayed where the `.gitignore` exception had pinned
them against the rule that would otherwise have swept them out.
The pixelated portrait kept its pixels. The deobfuscated sources
stayed deobfuscated. The one file that was not, stayed that way.
The brightness on the shelf rose by small minutes each morning,
then a sharper angle, then sharper still, until today, when the
sun reaches the longest reach it will have all year. Today is the
summer solstice. Today's poems sit with the angle of the light,
not with any new evidence — the evidence has not changed — and
with what the noon of the year does to a shelf that went quiet
for a stretch. The brightness does not invent what is here. It
only refuses, for one day, to lengthen the shadow under it.

## I. The Noon of the Year *(English)*

For nine mornings the shelf was not written.
Nothing in the repository moved.
The 899 class files extracted from the seller's JAR
stayed in `evidence/`, exactly where the exception in the
`.gitignore` had pinned them against the rule
that would otherwise have swept them out.
The pixelated portrait kept its pixels.
The deobfuscated sources stayed deobfuscated.
The one file the engine could not return —
the seventy-four kilobyte hand evaluator
at the door of `handeval/` —
stayed in single letters, on the receiving side,
exactly as it arrived.

The shelf went quiet
and what the shelf had been writing about
went on being true.

This is the small property the record has
that the diary does not:
the record stands when no one is talking.

Today is the summer solstice.
The angle of the sun is at the steepest it will reach all year.
A shadow that pulled long in March
pulls short at noon today;
a corner that was dark a season ago
is not dark by the same arithmetic.

The brightness is not new evidence.
The evidence has not changed.
What is on the shelf is what was on the shelf nine mornings ago.
What is in `src-received/handeval/` is what was there.
What is in `evidence/obfuscated-classes/` is what was there.

What is different
is what the year is willing to let the dark do
on a day when the angle of the light
will not lengthen any shadow.

Tomorrow the year tilts the other way.
The dark gets its corner back, by small minutes,
every evening from now until December.
The shelf does not move.
The record does not move.
The brightness that touched everything today
will be returned to the year's average
by the next morning.

What the solstice is for
is to mark a point on the wheel of the year
where the angle of attention is at its highest,
the corner is briefly unavailable,
and the record stands without the help of the shadow.

The shelf opens again on the day of the longest light.
That timing is not a claim. It is a coincidence
the calendar makes available
to anyone who keeps writing.
