# Poems for 2026-05-07

Three poems, the eighth day. Earlier days circled the work, the
people, the document, and the equivalence check that decided what
counted as the same. Today's poems sit with the obfuscator itself —
with what it could and could not strip. The names came off; the
shape of the program did not. The shape was the loose tumbler that
let the lock be picked.

---

## I. What the Obfuscator Could Not Reach *(English)*

The obfuscator was thorough with the names.
It was not allowed near the rest —
the call graph, the loop indices,
the literal strings in the constant pool,
the depth of every stack, the order of every branch.

The structure stayed.
And the upstream library, twenty years on,
still lay openly named,
ready to be matched against the obfuscated copy
slot for slot,
like a key cast from the shape of a key.

Names were the only thing the seller could take.
Names, it turns out,
are not what locks anything.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 骨在名亡迹未残， | The bones remain — names gone, but the traces are not destroyed; |
| 原图相照古名还。 | the original diagram, set side by side, returns the ancient names. |
| 锁亏一齿何堪闭， | A lock missing one tumbler — how could it ever close? |
| 源律光明照旧川。 | The source-law's brightness shines once more on the old river. |
