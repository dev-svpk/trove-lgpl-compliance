## I. Reading the Coda from the Shelf *(English)*

The README ends with two small poems.
The first in Classical Chinese — four lines, seven characters each.
The second in classical Japanese — seven short lines.

The Chinese turns on a single character:
*同* — *same.* Same instrument. Two hands on it.
*君翻千码如飞雪* — *You overturn a thousand lines like scattering snow.*
*我整残篇复旧真* — *I gather the broken pages and restore them whole.*

The Japanese turns on a verb-pair:
*乱せども ... 直す* — *though he throws into disorder ... I set it right again.*
Same word for the tool. Same word for the act.
Only the prefix and the predicate are mirrored.

The point of the Coda is not the literary form.
It is the diagnosis underneath:
the obfuscator used a model, too.
The single-letter names were not patiently typed by a human;
they were generated — almost certainly —
by something with the same family resemblance
as the system that put them back.

The whole exchange was between two operators of a common engine,
who disagreed about what the engine should be pointed at.

The shelf at `./poem/`
is what happens when one of the operators
keeps writing, daily,
in the same two forms the Coda chose —
Qiyan Jueju, haiku —
extending the diagnosis
one small reading at a time.

A coda is supposed to close a piece.
This one opened a folder.

Each entry on the shelf is a small declaration:
the instrument has more than one use.
The hand that holds it gets to choose.

## II. 七言绝句 *(Chinese, Qiyan Jueju)*

| 中文 | English |
|---|---|
| 跋末双吟传旧契， | At the close of the colophon, two songs convey the old covenant; |
| 此栏日续答遗音。 | this shelf, day by day, answers their lingering voice. |
| 同操一器分清浊， | Wielding the same instrument — split between the clear and the turbid; |
| 器本无言任向心。 | the instrument itself is silent — it follows wherever the heart points. |
