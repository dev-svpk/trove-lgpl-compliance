Three poems for 2026-05-01
==========================

I. English — "Restitution"
--------------------------

A thousand classes wore
their shaved-head names:
a, b, c — the alphabet of erasure.
The seller called it source.
We called it bluff.

We fed the cipher back to itself,
gated each rename
on a token stream that had to match,
on bytecode that had to round-trip,
and ten unattended hours later
the names were home.

Not the code. The code never left.
The words we use to ask the code
what it does —
those came back.
That is what the license meant
all along.


II. Chinese — Qiyan Jueju (七言绝句)
----------------------------------

匿字千行掩本真，
商人售伪自欺心。
机精十夜还原识，
开源如月照寒林。

English translation:
    A thousand lines of nameless letters veil the underlying truth;
    the merchant sold a forgery and lied to his own heart.
    The machine-spirit, in ten nights, restores what once was known —
    the open source, like the moon, lights up the cold dark woods.


III. Japanese — Haiku
---------------------

新緑や
匿し名どもの
甦る

Romaji:
    shinryoku ya
    kakushi na domo no
    yomigaeru

English translation:
    Fresh new green —
    the names that had been hidden
    come back to life.
